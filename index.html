Here’s how a balanced meal can be structured:

Vegetables: Ideally, half of the plate should be filled with vegetables. Try to include a variety of colors and types, both raw and cooked, for a range of nutrients.

Proteins: About a quarter of the plate should consist of protein sources such as chicken, fish, eggs, tofu, or legumes (beans and lentils).

Complex carbohydrates: Another quarter of the plate should contain complex carbohydrates like whole grains (brown rice, quinoa, or whole grain pasta) or starchy vegetables (sweet potatoes).

Healthy fats: Incorporate healthy fats from sources like olive oil, avocado, or nuts. These fats are important for nutrient absorption and overall health.

Hydration: Accompany your meal with water, the best choice for staying hydrated.

A balanced meal helps you maintain nutritional balance, providing your body with the energy and nutrients it needs to function at its best. This approach can be adjusted based on your individual dietary needs and health goals.
